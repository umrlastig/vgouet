ICT senior scientist in Content-based Image Retrieval, Computer Vision and Multimedia, with current applications to cultural and natural Heritage

Short bio. Valérie Gouet-Brunet is research director (DR1) of the French Ministry of Ecology (MTES) and carries out her research in the research department of the French mapping agency (IGN - National Institute for Geographical and Forest Information), within the LaSTIG laboratory and the Paris-Est University. Within the ACTE research group, she is in charge of researches on the description by content, matching, structuring and indexing of large-scale and long-term multimedia collections, with a focus on images and applications to cultural and natural heritage. Until 2018, she has headed the IGN's MATIS laboratory (35 members) for 4 years, specialized in mathematics and computer science applied to photogrammetry, computer vision and remote sensing for multi-sensor and multi-source imaging. She obtained a PhD in Computer Vision in 2000 from the University of Montpellier II (France) on the area of color image matching with application to intermediate view synthesis, and an habilitation to conduct research at the Pierre and Marie Curie University (France) in 2008 on the area of content-based structuring of collections of still and animated images.c V. Gouet-Brunet has supervised more than fifty researchers and participated in or coordinated some twenty partnership projects of various kinds (French national ANR and FUI projects, European projects, bilateral industrial contracts, international research collaborations). Currently, she is coordinating the ALEGORIA project (French ANR 2018-2021), is a member of the steering committee of the European project CSA Time Machine (2018-2019), and member of the board of the Time Machine Organisation.
